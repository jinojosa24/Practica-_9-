Welcome to Celta de Vigo

Celta de Vigo is a professional furbo club based in Vigo, Galicia, Spain. The club competes in La Liga, the top tier of Spanish football. Founded in 1923, Celta de Vigo has a rich history and is known for its passionate fanbase and competitive spirit. The club's home games are played at the legendary Balaídos Stadium.

History of Celta de Vigo

Celta de Vigo was founded on March 23, 1923, and since then, it has become one of the most important football clubs in the region of Galicia. The club has experienced various periods of success, including multiple stints in La Liga, the Spanish top division. Celta's greatest achievement came in the 2000s when they qualified for European competitions. Throughout its history, the club has earned the respect of fans and rivals alike for its strong youth development program and dedication to football excellence.

Balaídos Stadium

Balaídos Stadium, located in Vigo, is the home of Celta de Vigo. The stadium has a seating capacity of approximately 29,000 spectators. It was inaugurated in 1928 and has undergone several renovations over the years to keep up with modern standards. The stadium's atmosphere is electric during matchdays, with passionate Celta fans known for creating an intimidating environment for visiting teams.
